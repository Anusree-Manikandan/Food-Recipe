🍅 Thakkali Rasam (Tomato Rasam) Recipe 🍅

Servings: 4
Preparation Time: 10 minutes
Cooking Time: 20 minutes

🛒 Ingredients:

For Rasam:

  • 3 Ripe Tomatoes (chopped or crushed)
  • 2 cups Water
  • 1 tsp Tamarind Paste (or small lemon-sized tamarind soaked in warm water)
  • ½ tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1½ tsp Rasam Powder
  • ¼ tsp Black Pepper Powder (optional)
  • 1 tbsp Coriander Leaves (chopped)

For Tempering:

  • 1 tsp Ghee or Oil
  • ½ tsp Mustard Seeds
  • ½ tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 sprig Curry Leaves
  • 2 Garlic Cloves (crushed)
  • 1 Dry Red Chili
  • 1 pinch Asafoetida (Hing)

👩‍🍳 Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Base

In a bowl, mash or blend tomatoes to a pulp.
Soak tamarind in ½ cup warm water, extract the juice, and discard the pulp.
In a pot, add tomato pulp, tamarind extract, turmeric powder, rasam powder, black pepper, salt, and 2 cups water.

Step 2: Cook the Rasam

Bring the mixture to a gentle boil.
Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
Add chopped coriander leaves and turn off the heat.

Step 3: Tempering

Heat ghee in a small pan.
Add mustard seeds, let them splutter.
Add cumin seeds, crushed garlic, dry red chili, curry leaves, and hing.
Sauté for a few seconds and pour over the rasam.

Step 4: Serve Hot

Serve Thakkali Rasam with steamed rice, appalam, or as a soup.

⭐ Tips for Flavorful Rasam

✅ Do not overboil rasam; simmering retains the fresh taste.
✅ Use ghee for authentic taste and aroma.
✅ Crushed garlic enhances the flavor; you can skip it for a no-garlic version.
✅ Fresh coriander leaves add extra freshness.

Enjoy your Tomato Rasam! 🍅😋

🌶️ Milagu Rasam (Pepper Rasam) Recipe 🌶️

Servings: 4
Preparation Time: 10 minutes
Cooking Time: 15 minutes

🛒 Ingredients:

For the Rasam:

  • 1 tsp Tamarind Paste (or a small lemon-sized tamarind soaked in warm water)
  • 2 Ripe Tomatoes (crushed or chopped)
  • 2 cups Water
  • ½ tsp Turmeric Powder
  • ½ tsp Salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp Coriander Leaves (chopped)

For the Spice Powder:

  • 1 tsp Black Peppercorns
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 4-5 Garlic Cloves (crushed)
  • 1 Dry Red Chili
  • 1 tsp Toor Dal (optional, for extra flavor)

For Tempering:

  • 1 tsp Ghee or Oil
  • ½ tsp Mustard Seeds
  • 1 sprig Curry Leaves
  • 1 pinch Asafoetida (Hing)

👩‍🍳 Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Spice Powder

Dry roast black pepper, cumin seeds, garlic, red chili, and toor dal for 1-2 minutes.
Grind them into a coarse powder using a mortar and pestle or mixer grinder.

Step 2: Prepare the Rasam Base

Soak tamarind in ½ cup warm water, extract the juice, and discard the pulp.
In a pot, add crushed tomatoes, tamarind extract, turmeric powder, salt, and 2 cups of water.
Bring to a gentle boil and let it simmer for 5 minutes.

Step 3: Add the Spice Powder

Add the ground spice mix to the rasam.
Simmer for 2-3 minutes (do not overboil).
Turn off the heat and add chopped coriander leaves.

Step 4: Tempering

Heat ghee in a small pan.
Add mustard seeds, let them splutter.
Add curry leaves and hing, sauté for a few seconds.
Pour the tempering over the rasam.

Step 5: Serve Hot

Serve Milagu Rasam with steamed rice, appalam, or as a soup.

⭐ Tips for Perfect Milagu Rasam

✅ Use freshly ground pepper for the best flavor.
✅ Do not overboil; simmering enhances the taste.
✅ Ghee gives an authentic aroma; avoid refined oils.
✅ Add more garlic for extra health benefits.

Enjoy your Milagu Rasam! 🌶️🔥🍛
