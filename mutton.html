🐑 Mutton Chukka Recipe 🐑

Servings: 4
Preparation Time: 15 minutes
Cooking Time: 45 minutes

🛒 Ingredients:

Basic Things:

  • 500g Mutton (cut into small pieces)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 Tomatoes (pureed or finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 2-3 Green Chilies (slit)
  • 1 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tbsp Coriander Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tbsp Salt (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tbsp Coriander Leaves (for garnish)

For Tempering:

  • 2 tbsp Oil (or Ghee for extra flavor)
  • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds (optional)
  • 1 stick Cinnamon
  • 2-3 Cloves
  • 2-3 Cardamom Pods
  • 1 sprig Curry Leaves

👩‍🍳 Instructions:

Step 1: Tempering

Heat oil (or ghee) in a heavy-bottomed pan.
Add mustard seeds, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom pods, and curry leaves.
Let them splutter and release their aroma.

Step 2: Cook Mutton

Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
Add the mutton pieces and cook until they turn white and slightly browned.

Step 3: Add Tomatoes and Spices

Add pureed or chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt.
Cook until the tomatoes become soft and oil starts to separate from the masala.

Step 4: Slow Cook the Mutton

Add enough water to just cover the mutton (for cooking) and let it cook on medium heat for about 30 minutes or until the mutton becomes tender. Stir occasionally.
If you prefer a dry consistency, cook uncovered to allow the gravy to reduce.

Step 5: Finish and Garnish

Once the mutton is cooked and tender, check for seasoning and adjust salt/spices if needed.
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Step 6: Serve

Serve the Mutton Chukka with rice, chapati, or paratha.

⭐ Tips for Perfect Mutton Chukka

✅ Marinate the mutton with ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, and salt for about 30 minutes before cooking to tenderize and enhance the flavors.
✅ For extra tenderness, cook the mutton in a pressure cooker for 2-3 whistles before simmering.
✅ Use fresh spices for a more authentic and aromatic flavor.
✅ You can also add coconut milk towards the end for a creamy texture if preferred.

Enjoy your delicious Mutton Chukka! 🍖😋

🐑 Mutton Malai Varuval (Mutton Shami Kebab Style) 🍢

Servings: 4
Preparation Time: 20 minutes
Cooking Time: 45 minutes

🛒 Ingredients:

Basic Things:

  • 500g Mutton (boneless, cut into cubes)
  • 2 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 2 tbsp Yogurt
  • 2 tbsp Lemon Juice
  • 1 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tbsp Coriander Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala Powder
  • ½ tsp Fennel Seeds
  • 1 tbsp Peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1-2 Green Chilies (slit)
  • 1-2 tbsp Curry Leaves
  • 1 tbsp Salt (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tbsp Coriander Leaves (finely chopped for garnish)

For Tempering:

  • 2 tbsp Oil (or Ghee for a richer flavor)
  • 1 Onion (thinly sliced)
  • 1 tsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1-2 tbsp Coconut Milk (optional for richness)

👩‍🍳 Instructions:

Step 1: Marinate the Mutton

In a mixing bowl, combine mutton, ginger-garlic paste, yogurt, lemon juice, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, salt, and peppercorns.
Mix well to coat the mutton evenly with the marinade. Let it marinate for 30-60 minutes (for deeper flavor).

Step 2: Prepare the Spice Paste

In a small pan, dry roast fennel seeds, cumin seeds, and green chilies for 1-2 minutes until aromatic.
Grind the roasted spices into a fine paste by adding a little water. Set aside.

Step 3: Cook the Mutton

Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
Add the marinated mutton and cook on medium heat for 10-15 minutes until the mutton is browned.
Add the prepared spice paste to the pan and cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Step 4: Simmer the Mutton

Add a little water to the pan (just enough to cook the mutton). Cover and cook on low heat for 30 minutes or until the mutton becomes tender.
If you like the gravy to reduce, cook it uncovered until it thickens to a dry consistency.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Once the mutton is cooked, adjust the seasoning and consistency.
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and curry leaves.
Optionally, you can add coconut milk towards the end for extra creaminess.

Step 6: Serve

Serve Mutton Malai Varuval hot with rice, chapati, or paratha.

⭐ Tips for Perfect Mutton Malai Varuval

✅ For tender mutton, you can cook the mutton in a pressure cooker for 2-3 whistles before simmering.
✅ Fennel seeds and cumin seeds are key for authentic flavor, so don’t skip them!
✅ For a spicy kick, you can add more green chilies or increase the chili powder.
✅ The coconut milk will add a rich texture if you prefer a creamier version of the dish.
✅ If you want crispy edges, you can sear the mutton pieces in oil after they’re cooked.

Enjoy your delicious Mutton Malai Varuval! 🍖🔥
