🌿 Lady’s Finger Poriyal (Vendakkai Poriyal) Recipe 🌿

Servings: 4
Preparation Time: 10 minutes
Cooking Time: 15 minutes

🛒 Ingredients:

  • 250g Lady’s Finger (chopped)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 1-2 Green Chilies (slit)
  • ½ tsp Turmeric Powder
  • ½ tsp Red Chili Powder (optional)
  • 1 tsp Salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp Grated Coconut (optional)

For Tempering:

  • 1 tbsp Oil (Coconut oil preferred)
  • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • ½ tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 tsp Urad Dal
  • 1 sprig Curry Leaves
  • 1 pinch Asafoetida (Hing)

👩‍🍳 Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Lady’s Finger

Wash and completely dry the lady’s finger using a cloth or paper towel.
Chop into small pieces to avoid stickiness.

Step 2: Tempering

Heat oil in a pan.
Add mustard seeds, let them splutter.
Add cumin seeds, urad dal, curry leaves, and hing.
Sauté for a few seconds until dal turns golden.

Step 3: Cooking the Poriyal

Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn soft.
Add lady’s finger and sauté for 2-3 minutes on medium heat.
Sprinkle turmeric powder, salt, and red chili powder.
Stir well and cook on low heat for 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
Once the stickiness reduces, add grated coconut and mix well.

Step 4: Serve Hot

Serve Vendakkai Poriyal with sambar, rasam, or curd rice.

⭐ Tips for Perfect Lady’s Finger Poriyal

✅ Always dry the lady’s finger completely before chopping to reduce stickiness.
✅ Cook on low flame without covering the pan to avoid it from becoming mushy.
✅ Adding lemon juice or a little curd while cooking reduces stickiness.
✅ Grated coconut enhances taste, but you can skip it.

Enjoy your Vendakkai Poriyal! 🌿🍛😋

🥔 Potato Poriyal (Urulai Kizhangu Poriyal) Recipe 🥔

Servings: 4
Preparation Time: 10 minutes
Cooking Time: 20 minutes

🛒 Ingredients:

  • 3 Medium Potatoes (peeled & diced into small cubes)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 1-2 Green Chilies (slit)
  • ½ tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • ½ tsp Garam Masala (optional)
  • 1 tsp Salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp Grated Coconut (optional)

For Tempering:

  • 1 tbsp Oil (Coconut oil or any cooking oil)
  • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • ½ tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 tsp Urad Dal
  • 1 sprig Curry Leaves
  • 1 pinch Asafoetida (Hing)

👩‍🍳 Instructions:

Step 1: Cook the Potatoes

Boil the potatoes until they are fork-tender but not mushy. Drain and keep aside.
Alternatively, you can pressure cook them for 1-2 whistles or sauté raw potatoes for extra crispiness.

Step 2: Tempering

Heat oil in a pan.
Add mustard seeds, let them splutter.
Add cumin seeds, urad dal, curry leaves, and hing.
Sauté for a few seconds until the dal turns golden brown.

Step 3: Cooking the Poriyal

Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn soft.
Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Stir well.
Add the boiled potatoes and mix gently without mashing them.
Cook for 5-7 minutes on low flame, stirring occasionally.
Add garam masala (optional) for extra flavor.
If using, add grated coconut and mix well before turning off the heat.

Step 4: Serve Hot

Serve Potato Poriyal with sambar, rasam, curd rice, or chapati.

⭐ Tips for Perfect Potato Poriyal

✅ For a crispy version, fry the potatoes instead of boiling.
✅ Use coconut oil for an authentic South Indian flavor.
✅ Add a little lemon juice at the end for a tangy taste.
✅ Avoid overcooking potatoes to prevent them from becoming mushy.

Enjoy your Urulai Kizhangu Poriyal! 🥔🍛😋
