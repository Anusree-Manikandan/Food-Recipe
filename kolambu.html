🍛 Puli Kuzhambu (Tamarind Curry) Recipe 🍛

Servings: 4
Preparation Time: 10 minutes
Cooking Time: 25 minutes

🛒 Ingredients:

For the Puli Kuzhambu (Curry):

  • 2 tbsp Sesame Oil (Nallennai)
  • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • ½ tsp Fenugreek Seeds
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 sprig Curry Leaves
  • 2-3 Garlic Cloves (crushed)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 Tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1½ tbsp Sambar Powder (or Kuzhambu Milagai Thool)
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • ½ tsp Red Chili Powder (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp Salt (adjust as needed)
  • 1 small lemon-sized Tamarind (soaked in 1.5 cups of warm water)
  • 1 cup Vegetables of choice (brinjal, drumstick, okra, or potatoes)

For Garnishing:

  • 1 tbsp Coriander Leaves (chopped)
  • 1 tsp Jaggery (optional, to balance flavors)

👩‍🍳 Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare Tamarind Extract

Soak tamarind in warm water for 10 minutes.
Squeeze and extract the juice. Discard the pulp.

Step 2: Prepare Kuzhambu Base

Heat sesame oil in a pan.
Add mustard seeds, let them splutter.
Add fenugreek seeds, cumin seeds, and curry leaves.
Add crushed garlic and sauté until aromatic.
Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.

Step 3: Add Spices & Vegetables

Add turmeric powder, sambar powder, coriander powder, chili powder, and salt.
Mix well and cook for 2 minutes.
Add vegetables (if using okra, sauté separately first to remove stickiness).
Pour in the tamarind extract and mix well.

Step 4: Simmer & Thicken

Let the kuzhambu boil on medium flame for 10-15 minutes until it thickens.
Add jaggery if needed to balance the tanginess.

Step 5: Serve Hot

Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
Serve hot with steamed rice, dosa, or idli.

⭐ Tips for Perfect Puli Kuzhambu

✅ Use sesame oil for authentic taste and aroma.
✅ Adjust tamarind quantity based on sourness preference.
✅ Brinjal, drumstick, and okra are the best veggies for this dish.
✅ Jaggery balances the tanginess for a well-rounded flavor.

Enjoy your Puli Kuzhambu! 🍛😋

🥛 Mor Kuzhambu (Buttermilk Curry) Recipe 🥛

Servings: 4
Preparation Time: 10 minutes
Cooking Time: 20 minutes

🛒 Ingredients:

For the Kuzhambu (Curry)

  • 1 cup Thick Curd/Yogurt
  • 1½ cups Water
  • ½ tsp Turmeric Powder
  • ½ tsp Salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 cup Vegetables of choice (Ash gourd, pumpkin, or ladies’ finger)

For the Spice Paste:

  • 2 tbsp Grated Coconut
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 2 Green Chilies
  • 1 tbsp Toor Dal or Chana Dal (soaked for 10 minutes)
  • 1 tsp Ginger (chopped)

For Tempering:

  • 1 tbsp Coconut Oil or Sesame Oil
  • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • ½ tsp Fenugreek Seeds
  • 1 sprig Curry Leaves
  • 1 Dry Red Chili
  • 1 pinch Asafoetida (Hing)

👩‍🍳 Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Spice Paste

Soak toor dal/chana dal in warm water for 10 minutes.
Grind coconut, cumin seeds, green chilies, ginger, and soaked dal with little water to make a smooth paste.

Step 2: Cook the Vegetables

In a pan, cook chopped vegetables with a little water, salt, and turmeric powder until soft.
If using ladies' finger, sauté separately in oil to remove stickiness.

Step 3: Prepare the Kuzhambu

In a bowl, whisk curd/yogurt with water until smooth.
Add the ground spice paste and mix well.
Pour this mixture into the cooked vegetables and heat on a low flame (do not boil).
Stir continuously and turn off the flame once it is warm.

Step 4: Tempering

Heat coconut oil in a small pan.
Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
Add fenugreek seeds, curry leaves, dry red chili, and asafoetida.
Pour this tempering over the Mor Kuzhambu.

Step 5: Serve Hot

Serve Mor Kuzhambu with steamed rice, appalam, or kootu.

⭐ Tips for Perfect Mor Kuzhambu

✅ Do not boil the curd mixture, or it will curdle. Heat gently.
✅ Use fresh curd for the best taste. If the curd is sour, adjust salt accordingly.
✅ Coconut oil gives authentic South Indian flavor.
✅ Ash gourd, pumpkin, or ladies’ finger are the best vegetables for this dish.

Enjoy your Mor Kuzhambu! 😋🥛

🍛 South Indian Sambar Recipe 🍛

Servings: 4-5
Preparation Time: 15 minutes
Cooking Time: 30 minutes

🛒 Ingredients:

For Cooking Dal:

    ½ cup Toor Dal (Pigeon Pea Lentils)
    2 cups Water
    ¼ tsp Turmeric Powder
    1 tsp Oil

For Sambar:

  • 1 tbsp Tamarind (soaked in ½ cup warm water)
  • 1 Onion (chopped)
  • 1 Tomato (chopped)
  • 1 cup Vegetables (Carrot, Beans, Drumstick, Brinjal, Radish, or Pumpkin)
  • 1½ tsp Sambar Powder
  • ½ tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1½ cups Water

For Tempering:

  • 1 tbsp Oil or Ghee
  • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • ½ tsp Cumin Seeds
  • ¼ tsp Fenugreek Seeds
  • 2 Dry Red Chilies
  • 1 sprig Curry Leaves
  • 1 pinch Asafoetida (Hing)
  • 1 tbsp Coriander Leaves (chopped)

👩‍🍳 Instructions:

Step 1: Cook the Dal

Rinse toor dal and add it to a pressure cooker.
Add water, turmeric powder, and 1 tsp oil.
Pressure cook for 3-4 whistles until the dal is soft and mushy.
Mash the dal well and keep it aside.

Step 2: Cook the Vegetables

Heat oil in a pot and sauté onions until soft.
Add tomatoes and cook until mushy.
Add chopped vegetables and sauté for 2 minutes.
Add sambar powder, turmeric, and salt.
Pour 1½ cups water and cook until vegetables turn soft.

Step 3: Add Dal & Tamarind Extract

Extract juice from soaked tamarind and add it to the vegetables.
Add the cooked dal and mix well.
Simmer for 5-10 minutes until the sambar thickens.

Step 4: Prepare Tempering

Heat ghee or oil in a small pan.
Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, dry red chilies, curry leaves, and hing.
Pour the tempering into the sambar and mix well.
Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.

Step 5: Serve Hot

Serve Sambar with steamed rice, idli, dosa, or vada.

⭐ Tips for Perfect Sambar

✅ Use toor dal for authentic taste. You can also mix moong dal for a lighter version.
✅ Tamarind should not be added before vegetables are cooked, as it slows down the cooking.
✅ Use homemade sambar powder for the best taste.
✅ Drumstick, carrot, radish, and brinjal give the best flavor.

Enjoy your homemade Sambar! 🍛😋
